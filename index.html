Key Structural Changes at a Glance

Old Approach: Feature-Based New Approach: Task-Oriented Lifecycle
Organized by broad features and concepts (e.g., "Key features," "Common terms"). Organized by a clear, sequential lifecycle (e.g., "Planning," "Setup," "Recovery").
Mixed Content: Conceptual overviews were intermingled with procedural sections. Centralized Reference: Foundational concepts and a workflow overview are consolidated at the beginning.
Unstructured Flow: Less intuitive for new users who needed a linear implementation process. Logical Progression: The structure follows a natural path for new users and is easy to follow.
Deeply Nested: Specific service content was often at the bottom, disconnected from the main workflow. Clear Grouping: All related tasks for a stage (e.g., deploying Exocompute) are logically grouped under a single, high-level section.
Lower Readability: A long, flat list of topics made it difficult to quickly scan for information. Enhanced Readability: The hierarchical structure makes it easy to scan and quickly understand the relationship between topics.
